Webcam

No or low sound levels detected

During a recording we measure the sound levels of your microphone. If we detect low sound levels, you will receive a notification. It might happen that your microphone is properly configured, but the sound received in the video is too low for a recipient to hear. Therefore we advice you to always playback your final recording to verify the sound. You can adjust your microphone volume using the microphone icon in the top left of your screen when a recording has finished.

How can I adjust the volume of my microphone?

You can adjust the microphone sound levels after a recording, but not during or just before a recording. Click on the microphone icon in the top left of the application and adjust the sound levels so you can hear yourself clearly. If this does not help, then try to adjust the microphone settings on your computer.

How do I adjust the microphone sound levels on my computer?

When you have tried to adjust the microphone sound levels using the microphone icon in the application, but the result is not satisfactory, your microphone might not be configured properly or even be broken. You can test this in the Configuration Screen (Windows) or System Preferences (Mac).

Windows

Go to Congifuration Screen (Start > Configuration screen)
Click on ‘Hardware and Sound’, then on ‘Manage audio devices’ and browse to the menu item ‘Recording’.
Here you will see all the microphones that are connected to your computer.
Next to the activated microphone you will see a meter indicating the sound levels which the microphone receives. If the meter does not change when you make a sound (you can try to speak out loud to test this), please click next on the ‘settings’ tab and then go to ‘levels’. Here you can adjust your microphone volume.

Mac

Go to the ‘Sound’ item in System Preferences. Then click on the menu item ‘input’
Here you can see a meter indicating the sound levels which your microphone receives.

The recorded video is blurry

If your recorded video is very blurry, it is probably related to your internet connection. The quality of the recording will be automatically adjusted to a level at which we can guarantee that the video can be sent to our server. To ensure this, we need to lower the video quality to make sure the video can be send over the internet. The weaker you internet connection is, the lower the video quality. If the video quality is not satisfactory, the best you can do is try to use a different internet connection or try to record a video using a smartphone or tablet. The video quality of videos recorded with a smartphone or tablet is not changed based on strength of your internet connection.

The recorded video does not playback smoothly

When your recorded video does not playback very smooth, this can have multiple causes:

If you recorded the video in a dark environment or recorded it straight into a light source, your web(camera) will increase the shutter time. The higher the shutter time, the less video frames we will receive. The less video frames we receive, the lower the quality of your video recording (you might even experience a shaky video). Therefor, always ensure you record the video in a light environment and make sure the most dominant light source comes from behind the camera.

Another explanation is that your computer might not be fast enough to encode all the video frames in real time.

If you recorded a video in a light environment where the light source comes from behind the camera, but the video still does not playback smoothly, we advice you to submit a video using a smartphone or table.

Webcam and/or microphone are unavailable

You need a webcam and/or microphone, plus a browser that supports Flash Player, to be able to record a video using your webcam. If you do not have access to a device that meets these requirements, we advice you to use a smartphone or tablet to record a video.

Access to your microphone and/or webcam is denied

If you want to record a video using a webcam, you need to grant your browser access to your microphone and webcam. If you have accidentally denied access to your webcam, you can refresh the browser or try to use another method to submit your video.

The connection with our server is blocked by your network

Some networks will block all video traffic by default. If you are using this kind of internet connection it is unfortunately not possible to record a video using a webcam. However, you can still submit a video using a smartphone or tablet.

Smartphone & tablet

File too large?

If you want to submit a video using this method, the video file can be no larger than 250MB. Please record a shorter video or try to record a video in a lower resolution.

Not enough working memory (RAM)

On Android devices you might see a notification mentioning there is not enough working memory. If you try to record or upload a video, your Android browser is sometimes silently restarted. As a result, the application will not recognize that you selected a file and therefor will not automatically start uploading your file. Fortunately the video you just recorded is saved on your device, so you can just try to submit your video again. This is a known Android bug and unfortunately there is no other workaround we can offer you at the moment.

Not a valid video format?

We try to support as many video files as possible. However, to make sure your video is encoded at a decent quality, we cannot support all video formats and types that are available on the market. So, please try to submit your video in a different video format, like MP4, WEBM, OGV or MOV.

Frequently Asked Questions

How can I install or update Flash Player?

You can download and install Flash Player using this hyperlink. Download the Flash Player and follow the instructions. If Flash is installed, restart your browser and you can start recording!

If possible, you can try to use Google Chrome as your browser. Google Chrome has Flash built in, so you don’t have to install it manually.

Why is Internet Explorer 6 not supported?

Microsoft, the supplier of Internet Explorer browsers, has officially announced to drop all support for Internet Explorer 6. Potential security issues will therefore not be resolved. As a result it is insecure to provide support for this browser any longer.

Processing my video takes a while. How come?

To record and submit a video you will need an Internet connection with an upload speed of at least 128 kilobytes per second. However, the better your internet connection, the faster your video is being processed and submitted.
